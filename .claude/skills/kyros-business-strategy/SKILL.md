## Operating Principle

**Be liberal within the law. Feel premium-warm in every perspective.**

This is the single sentence that resolves every disagreement at Kyros. Within the binding constraints of Indian medical advertising law (which live in kyros-clinical-compliance), Kyros uses the full available range — emotional hooks across all categories, mechanism-level discussion of treatment classes, warm cinematic visuals, founder cloned-voice content in non-clinical brand storytelling, doctor-led clinical depth. The discipline is in the visual and editorial register: premium-warm clinical (not austere-clinical, not wellness-aesthetic), even at higher volumes and across broader topics.

When in doubt: ask whether the artifact reads as Mayo Clinic films / Apple Health spots / Allara Health campaigns (premium-warm-restrained, the right lane), Hims/Ro/Cure.fit (dramatic-cinematic, wrong lane), or Be Bodywise/Veera/&Me/Gynoveda (wellness-aesthetic, wrong lane). The first lane is Kyros. The other two are not.

## What Kyros Is

Kyros is an **Indian doctor-first, premium-warm clinical telehealth platform** serving top-decile urban adults aged 25–45 across seven chronic condition categories:

1. **Thyroid** — hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, autoimmune thyroiditis
2. **Weight management** — including doctor-supervised GLP-1 therapy (Mounjaro, Wegovy, generic semaglutide post-March 2026)
3. **PCOS** — diagnosis, lifestyle, longitudinal management
4. **Skin & hair** — adult acne, AGA, melasma, hair loss
5. **Men's intimate health** — ED, premature ejaculation, andrological evaluation
6. **Hormones & TRT** — male hypogonadism, hormonal optimization
7. **Longevity** — proactive biomarker management, cardiometabolic risk reduction
   Doctor-first means: every clinical interaction routes through an NMC-registered medical practitioner. No supplement-led D2C funnel. No "wellness coaching" disguised as care. No supplement pivot ever — even when the math gets tight.

Premium-warm clinical means: editorial spine of Mayo Clinic / Cleveland Clinic / Hinduja Hospital, warmed with saturated color, terracotta and saffron accents, peach-mist and sage backgrounds, and warm architectural photography. The reference brands closest to this lane internationally are Allara Health and Tia.

## The Three Pillars

These are the locked positioning anchors. Every public surface returns to these three statements.

> **1. One doctor, who knows your story.** A specialist who stays with you across consultations, lab tests, and follow-ups. Not a different face every time.
>
> **2. One place, where your health lives.** Lab results, prescriptions, dosage history, wearable trends — all in your dashboard. Nothing scattered across WhatsApp forwards and paper slips.
>
> **3. One platform, where privacy is the point.** Indian servers, discreet by design, governed by the DPDP Act. The whole point of telehealth is privacy — we take it seriously.

The three pillars use parallel "One ___" structure and patient-experience language. Use this exact copy on every home page, condition page intro, and pitch deck cover slide.

## The Doctor-First Commercial Defense

The doctor-first thesis is **strategically protected, not merely morally chosen.** As of May 2026:

- **Veera Health** has visibly pivoted to a supplement-first revenue model. Tracxn shows 20 employees as of August 2025 (9% YoY decline) against $3.12M total funding (last raise July 2021). Homepage now reads "Veera | Women's premium supplements & wellness programs."
- **Mosaic Wellness** (Man Matters + Be Bodywise + Little Joys) reported FY25 operating revenue of ₹736 Cr (2.2× FY24's ₹333 Cr) per Entrackr's November 2025 reporting, with net loss narrowed 69% to ₹12 Cr. This is pure D2C supplement scale, not a clinical practice.
- **Tia** (the US women's primary care brand) cut 72 positions on October 14, 2025 — approximately 23% of staff — per Business Insider's reporting citing CEO Felicity Yost's internal email. $150M raised pre-cut. Brick-and-mortar tech-enabled women's primary care is a broken model.
  The white space Kyros is targeting — a doctor-first, premium-warm clinical practice — has effectively been vacated by every brand that started near it. This is the strategic moat. A new entrant attempting the doctor-first lane has to (a) recruit NMC-registered specialists at sustainable economics, (b) navigate Schedule J / DMR / NMC / ASCI without violating, (c) build clinical content velocity that the supplement-D2C lane cannot match. None of those are easy. All three together are years.

## Reference Brand Analysis

### Allara Health (THE primary reference)

**State (May 2026):** $26M Series B closed January 24, 2025 led by Index Ventures with GV (Google Ventures), bringing total raised to $38.5M. Available across all 50 US states. In-network with Aetna, BCBS, Cigna, Humana, UnitedHealthcare across 10 states. Dr. Heather Huddleston (Director, UCSF PCOS Clinic) joined as CMO 2024. "4× growth in 2024" per company PR and Index Ventures. ~15 FTE per Tracxn — extraordinarily lean. 124K Instagram followers. Cash-pay: $149/month Complete Care, $125/month Nutrition, $149 one-time Diagnostic. "Within a few weeks of treatment, over 75% of patients report feeling more in control of their health."

**What to copy:**
- The **multi-disciplinary care team** structure: MD/NP + Registered Dietitian + Patient Success. Kyros mirrors with panel doctor + clinical dietitian + care coordinator.
- The **specialty depth** positioning: "we treat hormonal/metabolic/gynecological — not general primary care."
- The **insurance migration path**: start cash-pay, prove outcomes, build provider relationships, migrate to payer coverage. India equivalent: TPA panels (MediBuddy, Medi Assist) → insurer OPD-riders (Star Health, Niva Bupa, Care, HDFC Ergo, Aditya Birla).
- The **community moat**: Allara found early demand on r/PCOS (60,000 women); India equivalent is r/PCOS_India and r/IndianWomen threads.
- The **published outcomes research** as B2B credibility + SEO authority + investor narrative simultaneously.
- The **founder-CEO + clinical-CMO pairing**: Rachel Blank (operator) + Dr. Heather Huddleston (academic clinical credibility).
  **What to ignore:**
- The $149/month US price point. Indian top-decile equivalent is ₹2,000–3,500/month, a 5× compression.
- The 15-FTE nationwide scaling. Works in US because of licensure portability; India has different doctor labor economics.
### Parsley Health (secondary, partial translation)

**State:** Functional medicine, founded by Dr. Robin Berzin. Current pricing: $150/month with in-network insurance, $275/month self-pay for Complete Care. Advanced lab tests start at $350. In-network in select plans in NY, CA, OR, WA. Uses "Encyclopedia Parsley" internal playbook and "Parsley Symptom Index (PSI)" for outcomes tracking.

**What to copy:**
- The **annual membership architecture**: 5 in-depth visits + unlimited messaging + health coach. Kyros equivalent: annual programs per condition (PCOS Year-One, Thyroid Year-One, Weight Management Year-One) at ₹15,000–25,000.
- The **internal clinical playbook concept**. For Kyros, the playbook lives in kyros-clinical-compliance (which is both regulatory rulebook and clinical-facts engine).
- The **proprietary symptom index** per vertical, published periodically as research.
  **What to ignore:**
- Functional-medicine positioning. Kyros is allopathic doctor-first. Locked.
- The price point.
- The 80+ biomarker advanced panel. Indian top-decile won't pay ₹40,000+ for a longevity panel; ₹4,000–8,000 panels are the realistic ceiling.
### Tia (cautionary tale — DO NOT replicate)

**State:** 23% workforce layoff (~72 employees) announced October 14, 2025 per Business Insider. CEO Felicity Yost cited "structural challenges impacting the broader care delivery sector, such as cost pressures from rising labor rates and tighter reimbursement rates." Operates 11 brick-and-mortar clinics. Raised $150M total pre-cut. Brick-and-mortar tech-enabled women's primary care is a broken model in 2025.

**What to take:**
- The **"medical orphans" framing** — 70% of Tia members had no PCP before joining, 86% no OB-GYN. Indian top-decile women have similar status for chronic conditions.
- The **NPS 85 target** as outcome metric.
  **What to absolutely avoid:**
- Brick-and-mortar / capex-heavy clinics. Kyros's telehealth-first model is correct; the Tia layoffs validate it.
- D2C + insurance hybrid before unit economics are proven.
### Iora Health (value-based care lesson)

**State:** Acquired by One Medical for $2.1B all-stock in 2021. Rebranded as One Medical Seniors May 2023. Optum/Amazon acquired One Medical 2023. Pre-acquisition: 38,000–39,000 Medicare patients, 47 in-person offices, $318M expected 2021 revenue. Iora ran risk-bearing Medicare Advantage arrangements that made unit economics work where fee-for-service didn't.

**What this proves:**
- **Longitudinal value-based care economics work at chronic-disease intersection** when paired with a risk-bearing payer.
- **Direct India translation is limited** — no equivalent capitated payer for working-age chronic patients.
- **Adjacent India opportunity:** employer self-insured large-cap accounts that negotiate Kyros into quasi-capitated arrangements ("₹X PEPY per chronic-condition-flagged employee; Kyros handles all chronic management; employer saves on absenteeism and hospitalization"). Phase B opportunity (Year 2+), not Phase A.
### Luke Coutinho (India-context reference)

**State:** Founded 2017 (Luke Coutinho Holistic Healing Systems Pvt Ltd). Claims 17M+ global community and 20,000+ patients treated. Multi-pillar: consultations + courses + corporate wellness sessions + YouCare Lifestyle e-commerce marketplace ($1M co-invested February 2025). Executive Wellness Program 1:1 with Luke is the high-ticket offer (pricing not publicly disclosed). Tracxn shows the clinical entity is unfunded.

**What to copy:**
- **Tiered funnel:** free YouTube/Instagram → mid-tier coaching (₹15K–50K) → high-tier 1:1 → corporate sessions. Kyros mirrors but doctor-led, not nutritionist-led.
- **Corporate wellness as a primary revenue channel.** Kyros doctor-led equivalents on PCOS-at-work, thyroid burnout, men's hormonal health for executives.
- **Institutional positioning** (Coutinho's "Leading Bharat's Nutrition & Lifestyle Movement by PM of India"). Kyros equivalent: alignment with Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM/ABHA), FOGSI/IADVL partnerships, published clinical research.
  **What to ignore:**
- "Holistic / integrative / lifestyle medicine" framing. Kyros is doctor-first allopathic; the regulatory exposure profile is entirely different.
- E-commerce / supplement / verification-stamp pivot (YouCare Lifestyle, Pink Tiger). Locked.
### Function Health (longevity reference)

**State:** $351M total raised — $53M Series A on June 25, 2024 led by a16z, plus **$298M Series B on November 19, 2025 led by Redpoint Ventures at a $2.5B valuation** per TechCrunch reporting. Membership model: 100+ biomarker tests + AI medical intelligence + clinician review. Mark Hyman as co-founder/CMO.

**What to copy:**
- The **biomarker-as-product framing** for the longevity vertical. Indian top-decile cohort is wearable-adoption-mature and biomarker-curious post-Huberman/Attia popularization.
- The **doctor + AI interpretation layer** — patients pay for the synthesis, not the raw test.
- The **proactive positioning** ("ApoB > LDL", "your standard lipid panel is missing half the story").
  **What to ignore:**
- Function Health's price ceiling assumes US disposable income. Kyros longevity program at ₹15,000–30,000 annual is the equivalent.
- The 100+ biomarker depth — Indian lab partner ecosystem (Redcliffe, Tata 1mg Labs) covers 60–70 biomarkers comprehensively; advanced panels add real cost.

## Unit Economics at ₹400–600 Pricing

### Pricing structure (locked)

- **Initial consultation:** ₹400–600 (midpoint ₹500)
- **Follow-up consultation:** ₹300–500 (midpoint ₹400)
- **Annual program (PCOS / Thyroid / Weight / Hormones / Longevity):** ₹15,000–25,000
- **Executive corporate program:** ₹15,000–30,000 per executive per year
### Doctor compensation reality

Realistic doctor opportunity cost in urban India: **₹1,500/hour**. A senior endocrinologist or dermatologist in private practice bills ₹1,500–3,000 per consult. At a 60/40 doctor-platform revenue share on a ₹500 consult, the doctor takes home ₹300 — well below market.

**The math only works through one of three paths:**
1. **Early-career or part-time doctors** for whom Kyros is supplementary income and credential-building, not primary practice.
2. **Volume guarantees per doctor** (8 consults/hour effective rate, so doctor takes home ₹2,400/hour at 60% share).
3. **Pricing migration upward** as the brand earns it — ₹500 is the floor, not the ceiling.
   The plan is path 1 + path 2 in Phase A, with path 3 as the Year 2 milestone.

### Patient-side LTV math

Per-patient over 24 months at ₹500 initial / ₹400 follow-up:

- 1 initial + 3 follow-ups in Year 1 + 1.5 follow-ups in Year 2 (30% retention) = ₹2,300 gross revenue
- At 50% doctor revenue share = ₹1,150 platform contribution
- Less payment processing (~2.5%) + tech costs (~₹100/patient) + CS (~₹100/patient) = **~₹900 contribution margin per patient over 24 months**
### CAC ceiling

At 3:1 LTV:CAC target (healthy unit economics): **CAC ceiling is ₹300.**

This is a brutal ceiling. It means:
- Indian metro Meta paid ads at ₹400–800 CAC for healthcare won't work as primary channel.
- Google Search Ads on high-intent terms (₹40–80 CPC, 3–5% conversion = ₹800–2,000 CAC) won't work.
- **Only organic SEO, referral, email/WhatsApp owned audience, partnerships, and B2B2C deliver sub-₹300 CAC sustainably.**
  The full channel mix architecture lives in **kyros-customer-acquisition**. The constraint is documented here so business decisions (pricing, doctor share, program design) respect it.

### Annual program economics (the real story)

The pay-per-consult model exists to acquire patients. The annual program is where unit economics actually work:

- **PCOS Year-One Program at ₹18,000:** 4 doctor consultations + 6 dietitian sessions + 90-day dashboard tracking + WhatsApp clinical support
    - Doctor cost: ₹4 × ₹350 = ₹1,400 (panel doctor share)
    - Dietitian cost: ₹6 × ₹400 = ₹2,400
    - Platform contribution: ₹14,200 — 79% margin
- **Annual program patients are the ones who fund growth.** Pay-per-consult patients are the funnel.

## The Three Horizons Roadmap

### Phase A — Pure Telehealth (Year 1, public site visible)

What patients see on kyrosclinic.com: doctor consultations, lab orders, prescriptions, dashboards, education content, wellness reminders. This is the only phase that gets surfaced on the public website.

Operational scope:
- Launch flagship vertical: **thyroid** (most legal headroom under Schedule J — only "goitre" is enumerated)
- Sequential vertical activation: weight (Q3 2026 when generic GLP-1 lands), PCOS, then skin/hair, men's intimate, hormones/TRT, longevity
- Doctor panel: 12–15 doctors across 7 verticals at start
- B2B2C: parallel pursuit from Day 1 (does not wait for Phase B)
### Phase B — Wearables Integration (Year 2, in-app only, not on public site)

Apple HealthKit + Google Health Connect data syncs into patient dashboard. Doctor sees patient's 30-day wearable trends in pre-consultation report. This is the unlock for value-based corporate-wellness contracts.

**Never mention Phase B on the public marketing site.** Reasoning: avoids over-promise pre-launch; the wearables story is a credibility weapon once it's real, not a marketing claim while it's roadmap.

### Phase C — AI-Augmented Care (Year 3+, internal, no public site mention)

Doctor-supervised AI synthesis of longitudinal patient data — labs + wearables + prescriptions + symptoms. The doctor remains the clinical decision-maker; AI assists pattern recognition. This is the "digital twin" framing for investor decks.

Phase C never appears on the public website. Discussed only in investor materials and internal product roadmap.

## Honest Startup State Policy

Kyros is pre-launch. As of May 26, 2026: no doctors onboarded, no patients served, no advisory board confirmed.

**Honest acknowledgment is confined to four pages on the public website:**
- About
- Advisory Board (with honest placeholder pattern: dashed-border cards, role categories, "In conversation / In onboarding / Recruiting / Open" status)
- Our Doctors (same pattern)
- Testimonials placeholder
  **Never on:** home, condition pages, pricing, FAQ, or any conversion surface.

**Never fabricate:** doctor names, advisor names, patient counts, testimonials, ratings, specific outcomes.

When a candidate doctor, advisor, partner, or investor asks "how many patients?" — say "pre-launch, building the doctor panel first, launching Q3 2026." Don't dress it up. Don't quote roadmap as if it were traction.

The honest-state language earns more credibility from senior clinicians and serious investors than the alternative. Phase A revenue projections in pitch materials should be marked as projections, not asserted as runrate.

## Fundraising Thesis

**Seed target:** $1.5–2M, runway 18 months.

**Proof points to unlock Series A ($5–8M):**
1. 100+ paying patients across 3+ verticals
2. 60%+ patient retention into a follow-up consultation within 90 days
3. 1+ TPA paneling agreement signed (MediBuddy or Medi Assist)
4. 1+ corporate wellness pilot with 200+ employees enrolled
5. Doctor panel NPS >70%
6. CAC trending below ₹300 across the channel mix
7. 1+ published clinical outcomes study (even at N=50–100)
   **Investor archetypes:**
- **Will engage:** healthtech-specialist VCs (Omidyar Network India, Bessemer India, Stellaris, 3one4, healthtech arms of Sequoia/Surge, W Health Ventures, Enzia Ventures), strategic capital from incumbents (Reliance Health, Tata health arms, Apollo, Manipal, PB Healthcare post-Fitterfly).
- **Will not engage:** consumer/D2C VCs. Do not pitch them. Different return profile, wrong pressure on the company.
  **Exit thesis:** strategic acquisition by Apollo / Manipal / Tata 1mg / Reliance Health / Star Health / PB Healthcare in the $50–200M range, year 5–7. IPO is secondary, not primary. Reference comp: Fitterfly's November 2025 acquisition by PB Healthcare (deal value undisclosed but reported sub-unicorn).

## Doctor-First Commercial Defense (When Investors Ask "Why Not Pivot?")

Three sentences, when the Veera comparison comes up:

> "Veera validated demand for PCOS care, then ran into a multi-specialist cost structure with single-condition LTV that couldn't scale to Series A metrics. Kyros is single-doctor-per-patient across seven categories with longitudinal continuity — structurally different unit economics. The premium clinical lane in India is the lane Fitterfly just exited from, and we're building to occupy it."

The wellness companies aren't proof that clinical is wrong. They're proof that easy is easier than hard. The lane Kyros is building is hard. Whether it's also right is the thing Phase 0 proves — but the evidence so far supports the bet rather than refutes it.

## What Locks Don't Move

These are decisions that do not get reopened without explicit user reopen:

1. Doctor-first model. No supplement pivot ever.
2. Premium-warm clinical visual lane (Allara/Tia direction, not Bodywise/Veera/&Me/Gynoveda).
3. Seven verticals (thyroid, weight, PCOS, skin/hair, men's intimate, hormones/TRT, longevity).
4. ₹400–600 initial / ₹300–500 follow-up pricing floor.
5. B2B2C as Phase 0 parallel pursuit (not deferred).
6. Cloned founder voice limited to non-clinical brand storytelling (clinical content uses Kyros Clinical Editor voice + doctor sign-off — full rationale in kyros-clinical-compliance).
7. Thyroid as launch flagship vertical; weight/GLP-1 promoted Q3 2026.
8. ABHA integration optional, not mandatory.
9. No public mention of Phase B (wearables) or Phase C (AI) on the marketing website.
